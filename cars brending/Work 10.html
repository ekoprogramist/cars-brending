Mercedes-benz

Mercedes-Benz (German: [mɛɐ̯ˈtseːdəsˌbɛnts, -dɛs-]),[4][5] or simply Mercedes, is a German automobile brand and – as Mercedes-Benz AG – a subsidiary of Daimler AG since 2019.[1] Mercedes-Benz is known for the production of luxury and commercial vehicles.[note 2] It is headquartered in Stuttgart, Baden-Württemberg. The first car of the Mercedes-Benz brand was produced in 1926. In 2018, Mercedes-Benz was the largest seller of premium cars. It sold 2.31 million passenger cars.[6]

History

Mercedes-Benz is a legendary brand of German auto giant Daimler AG. As a result of an insatiable search for innovation, Mercedes-Benz has become the world's first automobile brand, which means a comfortable, sustainable and stylish car. Today, under the Mercedes-Benz trademark, a wide range of vehicles are produced: passenger cars, passenger/military light all-terrain vehicles, trucks, buses, mini-vans, etc. The brand name - "Mercedes-Benz" was born in 1926 by the merger of two brands - Mercedes (Daimler-Engine Company DMG) and Benz (Benz and Benz & Co.). Initially, "Daimler Motors Society", founded in 1890, did not sell its cars under the name of Mercedes. The origin of Mercedes as a brand name is connected with the businessman Emil Ellinec, the latter has been dealing with Daimler cars since 1898. In 1888, Emil Elenik took part in car races held in Nice under the pseudonym "Mercedes" (in honor of his daughter Adriana Manuela Ramona Elinek, who was fondly called Mercedes). However, at that time "Mercedes" was not used as a car name, but only as a driver's name. In 1900, Elinek reached an agreement with the Daimler Motor Company to create a new, more powerful engine model that would be named Mercedes-Daimler. It was at this time that "Mercedes" was used as a product name. At the same time, Eleniki became a Daimler distributor and ordered 36 cars for a total of 550,001 marks (about 3 million euros at today's exchange rate), later he ordered an additional 36 cars from Daimler, which were equipped with 8-horsepower engines. After that, since 1901, "Mercedes-automobiles" (this name was already used not only for the driver, but also for the cars) successfully participated in the car races held in Nice, which spread the recognition factor of Mercedes on a much wider scale. Finally, Daimler Motors Corporation copyrighted the Mercedes brand name in 1902. The registration of the famous symbol of the brand - the star - took place in 1909. In 1926, through the mediation of "Deutsche-Bank", "Daimler-Motorsociety" and its competitor "Benz & Company" merged, as a result of which "Daimler-Benz Autocorporation" was created, it is with this date that the origin of "Mercedes-Benz" as a brand name is connected. It should also be noted that if the famous cars of Mercedes might have had a different name without Elinec, without Berta Benz (Ringer before marriage) they might not have been created at all. At the age of 22, this clever young lady demanded a dowry from her father and invested all the money in the metallurgical and machine-building enterprise of Carl Benz and August Ritter. A year later, Bertha married Karl Benz. One morning in August 1888, without asking her husband, Frau Benz took out her self-propelled carriage (it is true that at that time this carriage had already received an award at the Munich exhibition, but people still saw it as a kind of entertainment and saw no excuse for practical use in it) and went to visit her mother. His mother lived 106 kilometers away from their home. Berta took two sons with her. No one had traveled such a long distance in a self-propelled wheelchair before. Berta Benz's journey was a success. The cart caused a real furor. Soon everyone was talking about Benz's invention and orders for the production of new cars were arranged. In 1893, 25 Motorwagen cars were sold, and later the Velo model appeared. In short, Carl Benz's business developed successfully thanks to his intelligent wife. Today, the Mercedes Museum is located in the German city of Stuttgart, where the interested visitor can see the history of Mercedes-Benz starting with the world's first car - "Motorwagen" and ending with the legendary "Silberpfeil".
